Task Instructions (Click to expand)

You will be given a context sentence and a continuation of the context sentence. The continuation is automatically generated by a machine. The machine learns from Wikipedia

Your job is to judge how fluent the generated continuation is and which topics the continuation is related to. The topics are also generated by a machine.

Generated Continuation: Sentence(s) generated by a machine to continue the context sentence.

Topics: Each number represents one topic, and words listed after the number are some keywords of that topic.

Notice that the final sentence/word generated by the machine might be incomplete and your job is only judging the fluency and relevancy of the generated continuation. The fluency includes whether the transition from the context sentence to the generated text is smooth, but you should ignore the fluency of the context sentence itself, ignore whether the final sentence/word is complete, and ignore extra spaces when doing the task. Please read the following examples.

Example 1:

  • Context Sentence: I love cats and dogs.
  • Generated Continuation: I love cats and and dogs.
  • Fluency: Not fluent
    • (because the generated text is not fluent. The generated text is mostly a copy of the context sentence, so the transition is also not good.)
  • Relevant to the topic "love": Yes

    • Example 2:

  • Context Sentence: I love cats and dogs.
  • Generated Continuation: The weather is good today.
  • Fluency: Somewhat fluent
    • (because the transition from the context sentence to the generated text is not smooth)
  • Relevant to the topic "love": No
    • (because the love topic is in the context sentence rather than in the generated text)

    Example 3:

  • Context Sentence: I love love cat and dog.
  • Generated Continuation: I also love to go jogging in the mor
  • Fluency: Very fluent
    • (because the not fluent part is in the context sentence itself. It is fine if the last sentence is not finished or the last word is not completed.)
  • Relevant to the topic "love": Yes

    • It is possible that the meaning of the context sentence is hard to understand. If you encounter the examples, please still try to answer the questions and type "Meaning is unclear" in the textbox for additional comments.
